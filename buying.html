Food Tip 1:

Don't shop on an empty stomach(you are more likely to buy food you don't need).

Food Tip 2:

If possible shop online, you will be less tempted to buy things not on your
list, it also enables you to look for money off vouchers

Food Tip 3:

Supermarkets sometimes have special discounts for certain groups such as icelands 10% off for pensioners when shopping on Tuesday's. Many retailers offer discounts for emergency/nhs workers, students and pensioners.

Food Tip 4:

Don't just go for well known brands, trade secret a lot of the big brand items are made by the same factories that
produce the supermarket own brands. So why not try out the supermarket value range.

Food Tip 5:

Meal planning and batch cooking may take some extra time but it can save you
money in the long-run. Take a
look at our making food section for some great meal ideas.

Food Tip 6:

If you find meal planning too difficult or end up spending on extras when shopping perhaps a meal delivery company would enable you to budget better.
Recommended companies like gousto and hello fresh can save you time on planning/shopping and you know exactly how much your food will cost each month.

Sell by/best before dates...

Food that has almost gone out of date or is just past it's sell by date is still perfectly fine to eat!Remember best before/sell by does not mean you cannot eat it after that date. There are many sites that sell discounted foods with a short shelf life but one of the highest rates is approved foods;

Approved foods
